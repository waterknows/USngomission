Founded in 1831, Wesleyan University is among the oldest of the numerous originally Methodist institutions of higher education in the United States. The student body is made up of approximately 2,700 full-time undergraduates and 200 graduate students. With a student-faculty ratio of 9:1, Wesleyan offers more than 900 courses in 39 departments and 46 major fields of study. The master of arts degree and the doctor of philosophy are regularly awarded in six fields of study. The more than 300 faculty members all teach undergraduates while remaining active as scholars and researchers.
