The Supportive Housing Network of New York, established in 1988, is a nonprofit membership organization with offices in New York City and Albany. The Network represents over 220 nonprofits that develop and operate supportive housing. As the only supportive housing membership organization in the state, the Network serves as a voice for the provider community. It's mission is to provide public education, training and advocacy for its non profit member organizations. The Network now includes almost every supportive housing provider in New York. It also includes over 80 affiliate and corporate partners. Thanks in part to the Network's advocacy efforts, New York is now home to over 43,000 units of supportive housing.
