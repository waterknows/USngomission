Bowdoin is an independent, nonsectarian, coeducational residential, undergraduate liberal arts institution founded in 1794. It is located in Brunswick, Maine, a town of 21,000 on the Maine coast. Study at Bowdoin leads to a bachelor of arts degree in one of over 40 departmental and interdisciplinary majors. Bowdoin enrolls approximately 1,710 students from across the country and around the world. Bowdoin faculty members are leading scholars, performers, researchers, and artists who live what they teach. They are here because they truly enjoy the give-and-take of teaching undergraduates.
