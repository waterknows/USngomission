Founded in 1924, Duke University works to provide a superior liberal education; to prepare future members of the learned professions for lives of skilled and ethical service; to advance the frontiers of knowledge and contribute boldly to the international community of scholarship; to promote an intellectual environment built on a commitment to free and open inquiry; to help those who suffer, cure disease, and promote health, through medical research and patient care; to provide educational opportunities, on and beyond our campuses; and to promote an appreciation for the range of human difference and potential, a sense of the obligations and rewards of citizenship, and a commitment to learning, freedom and truth. Duke enrolls 6,400 undergraduate and 7,262 graduate and professional students.
