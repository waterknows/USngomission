Founded in 1989, AIDS Research Alliance exists to develop a cure for HIV/AIDS, medical modalities to prevent new infections and better treatments for those living with HIV. The core areas of the AIDS Research Alliance non-profit research model include: independent clinical research, without regard to profit; clinical trials of innovative anti-HIV technologies and treatments which include vaccines, microbicides and other biomedical prevention technologies; the development of treatments for HIV-related conditions; and collaborations and strategic partnerships with other scientific organizations (including academic institutions, biotech and pharmaceutical companies and governmental agencies).
