Concordia College is an historically black, four-year, coeducational college, founded in 1922. Concordia is a private institution of the Lutheran Church-Missouri Synod (LCMS), where the Christian faith is taught from the Holy Scriptures and subscribed by the Lutheran Church-Missouri Synod. The mission of Concordia College is to educate students through Christ-centered education for lives of responsible and effective service in the church, community, and the world. To achieve this mission, Concordia engages its students in programs and activities which identify and meet their spiritual, academic, social, and physical needs. Challenging and rigorous academic programs lead to a Bachelor's degree in early childhood or elementary teacher education, a Bachelor of Science in Business Administration, or an Associate of Arts degree in general studies.
