Established in 1977, Dogs for the Deaf is a non-profit organization that rescues, trains and places dogs with people who have different disabilities and needs throughout the United States. Hearing Dogs, Autism Assistance Dogs, Program Assistance Dogs (dogs that work with teachers, counselors, and physicians), and Career Change Dogs are free to clients after a $50 application fee and refundable deposit.Out of town visitors and local residents can visit and learn more about Dogs for the Deaf by taking a free tour of the facility weekdays at 11 a.m. or 1 p.m. The address is 10175 Wheeler Road, Central Point, Ore., 97502. For more information visit www.DogsForTheDeaf.org or call 1-800-990-DOGS (3647).
