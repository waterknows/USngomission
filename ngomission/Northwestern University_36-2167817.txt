Founded in 1851, Northwestern University combines innovative teaching and pioneering research in a highly collaborative environment that transcends traditional academic boundaries. It provides students and faculty exceptional opportunities for intellectual, personal, and professional growth in a setting enhanced by the richness of Chicago. Northwestern University enrolls 8,497 undergraduate and 7,880 graduate students full-times. The mission of Northwestern University is to establish and enhance excellence in its academic and professional programs. The research program at Northwestern is a major component of university efforts, assuring institutional leadership in scientific discovery, intellectual inquiry, and creative performance.
