Founded in 1826, Lafayette College seeks to nurture the inquiring mind and to integrate intellectual, social, and personal growth. The College's curriculum is distinguished by the rare combination, on an undergraduate campus, of degree programs in the liberal arts and in engineering. Students who come to Lafayette may choose among a range of disciplinary and interdisciplinary courses and pursue the Bachelor of Arts degree in 32 fields or the Bachelor of Science degree in nine fields of science and four fields of engineering. The College enrolls approximately 2,382 students, and has a student/faculty ratio of 11:1.
