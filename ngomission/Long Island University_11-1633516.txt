Long Island University was chartered by the New York State Education Department in 1926 in Brooklyn, New York, as a nonsectarian, coeducational, privately supported university to provide effective and moderately priced education to people from all walks of life. One of the largest and most comprehensive universities in the country, it offers 590 undergraduate, graduate and doctoral degree programs and certificates, and educates more than 24,000 students on six campuses in Brooklyn, Brookville (C.W. Post), Southampton, Brentwood, Rockland and Westchester.
