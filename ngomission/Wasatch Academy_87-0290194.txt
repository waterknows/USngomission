Wasatch Academy was founded in 1875 by Duncan J. McMillan, a Presbyterian minister devoted to life improvement through education. Today, Wasatch Academy provides a nurturing community that empowers young men and young women to develop academically, socially, and morally, preparing them for college and for the challenges of living in the 21st century. Since the 1950's the school has attracted strong contingencies of students from throughout the world. Today, the school serves a student body of over 200, representing 28 countries and 22 states. Wasatch Academy's ability to provide outstanding educational opportunities to a richly diverse student body is the school's greatest asset.
